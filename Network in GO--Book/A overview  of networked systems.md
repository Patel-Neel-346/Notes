In the digital age, an increasing number of devices communicate over computer networks.
A computer network is a connection between two or more devices, or nodes, that allows each node to share data.

#### Choosing a network topology
The organization of nodes in a network is called its topology. A network’s topology can be as simple as a single connection between two nodes or as complex as a layout of nodes that don’t share a direct connection but are nonetheless able to exchange data.
##### Point-to-Point Topology
- **Meaning**: A direct connection between two devices. Data flows exclusively between them.
- **Real-World Use**:
  - **Telecommunication Links**: Dedicated leased lines between offices.
  - **IoT Device Communication**: Bluetooth or direct device-to-device data transfer.

##### Daisy Chain Topology
- **Meaning**: Devices are connected sequentially, one after another, forming a linear chain.
- **Real-World Use**:
  - **Small Industrial Networks**: Used in simple monitoring systems.
  - **LED Lighting Systems**: Strings of connected LEDs.


##### Bus Topology
- **Meaning**: All devices share a single communication line (bus) with terminators at both ends to prevent signal bounce.
- **Real-World Use**:
  - **Automotive Systems**: CAN (Controller Area Network) in vehicles.
  - **Legacy Ethernet Networks**: Early networking setups.

##### Ring Topology
- **Meaning**: Devices form a circular connection where data travels in one or both directions.
- **Real-World Use**:
  - **Telecommunication Networks**: SONET/SDH for redundancy and fault tolerance.
  - **Older LANs**: Token Ring systems.

##### Star Topology
- **Meaning**: All devices connect to a central hub or switch. Communication passes through this central node.
- **Real-World Use**:
  - **Home Networks**: Wi-Fi routers and connected devices.
  - **Office Networks**: Devices linked to a central switch.

##### Mesh Topology
- **Meaning**: Devices are interconnected, either fully (all devices connect to all others) or partially (some devices connect directly).
- **Real-World Use**:
  - **IoT Networks**: Smart home systems with sensors and lights.
  - **Wireless Networks**: Mesh Wi-Fi systems like Google Nest.
